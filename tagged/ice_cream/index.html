A Teenage Babysitter, a Fast No-Cook Meal Plan & Harry Potter

Tonight the Little Ladies will have their first experience with a teenage babysitter flying solo (their older step-brothers are away this week).  They are over-the-moon excited, and they have a whole list of things they’d like to do in the 4 whole hours their parents will be away.  Luckily for the babysitter, the list includes the 2nd installment in the Harry Potter series (Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets), so it should really be an easy gig.

With their father and I dining out, I decided to give myself a much-deserved break and let the kids order in.  What do children with familial hypercholesterolemia eat when ordering take-out anyway?  Or for that matter, what does any child whose parents strive to feed them a healthy (or at least healthier) diet order?  Well, we’ve figured a bunch of valid options, but tonight they’re having GASP! pizza, a fruit & veggie tray and root beer floats (the latter being a more benign version of the typical special treat served in ice cream parlors). 

Here is the plan:

Main Course: 

Pizza - Readers of this blog know that we typically have “Pizza Friday,” especially at lunch time during the school year when we mimic the school cafeteria and serve the same lunch item, only much healthier versions.  Check the archives for examples.  I do not subscribe to the thought that pizza has to have extra cheese and a load of meat (like sausage and pepperoni) to be yummy, nor do typically feel good about blowing the day’s allotment of saturated fat, sodium and calories in one sitting (on the rare occasions that I do it has to be on something extra special). Pizza can, in fact, be very healthy.

Tonight we will order a thin crust whole wheat margherita pizza from our local pizzeria, and request that the fresh mozzarella topping be reduced to half of what they normally serve.  The pizzeria also serves homemade sauce (without excess sugar or sodium) and uses extra virgin olive oil, so we’re good there.  The kids prefer their veggies on the side (not on) their slices, and that’s perfectly fine.  I, however, love my veggies piled sky high!

Beverage - Filtered water.  It’s what we typically drink with dinner every night.

Side Dish: 

Fruit & Cheese Platter - a simple-to-manage selection of dark cherries, watermelon slices (pre-cut by me), whole pluots and raw vegetables such as baby carrots, red and orange peppers and cucumbers and whatever else they pull out of the fridge.

And for the finale:

Dessert: 

Root Beer Floats - Using smaller glasses (like these from Crate and Barrel, only ours are 4 ounces), the root beer float will be made from Oogave Root Beer (click here for information on the brand) which contains agave as a sweetener, and a scoop of low-fat vanilla frozen yogurt.  One 12 ounce bottle can be split between 3 glasses, so it’s a lot of yum and not much yuck.

I have a feeling my kids are going to enjoy themselves tonight.  I have a feeling their parents will too.

The Soft Serve Fruit Co.

I don’t normally utilize this blog to write about things I don’t make myself, but good golly maybe I should start.  And hey, companies that are actually trying to make a fantastic, healthier product should be applauded and recognized publicly, right?

I was coming home from our yearly pediatric appointments with the Little Ladies (they had just braved having 8 vials of blood drawn each) when we spied The Soft Serve Fruit Co.  “What is this place?” I wondered.

Here’s what we learned:

The Soft Serve Fruit Co. uses only 3 ingredients in their product:

  • seasonal fruit
  • filtered water
  • a touch of cane sugar

End of story. And the products are non-dairy, free of fat, sodium, cholesterol and gluten.  A small cup of Soft Serve Fruit (which is actually a very generous serving size) is less than 90 calories, and it tastes fantastic.  The 3 of us left feeling gleeful and refreshed (we each had a serving of the mango soft serve — out of this world).

Toppings are available, and they’re often organic and free of nitrates, coloring, preservatives, high fructose corn syrup, etc. 

I wish more companies like The Soft Serve Fruit Co. existed.  Luckily for my lactose-intolerant husband, there is a location only a few short blocks from where he works. 

There are currently 3 locations in New York City.  Click here for further information (including contact info so you can harass the owners about opening a location near you).  :0) 

Note:  This post was not sponsored or influenced in any way other than by my taste buds and my desire to support companies that care about the products they serve.

Georgia Peach Vegan Soft-Serve

Brace yourselves for a series of peach posts, as we’re back from the South with more Georgia peaches than you can shake a stick at (although we left our favorite Georgia peach — our guest blogger Tiffany Ard — behind so she can keep on nerding it up without the distractions of New York City).  

The possibilities are endless in both the sweet and savory categories, and we hand-selected the best of the best, so bear with us as we obsess a little.

First up, Cousin Doug’s Georgia Peach Vegan Soft-Serve, inspired by a recipe in the book Lick It! Easy, Dairy-Free Recipes by Cathe Olson.

Cousin Doug has been dairy-free for many years now, so he is our go-to guy when my lactose intolerant husband is craving ice cream.  When we showed up on his doorstep it was a no-brainer to request peach soft-serve (simply freeze longer to get a scoopable consistency).

You will need:

  • 4 cups of peaches (2 cups roughly sliced and 2 cups diced)
  • 1/4 cup of filtered water
  • 14 ounces of coconut milk
  • 1/2 cup of granulated sugar or agave syrup (note:  1/3 cup would be perfect and that is what we’ll use next time)
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract

Bring peaches and water to a boil on medium-low heat.  Reduce heat to low, cover and simmer 5 to 8 minutes until peaches soften.  Cooking the peaches intensifies the flavor and makes the peaches very easy to peel.

Combine the coconut milk, sugar and vanilla extract in a blender.

Pour in the 2 cups of roughly sliced peaches and process until smooth.  Add 2 cups of diced peaches for texture.

Cover and chill in the refrigerator for at least 3 hours.  Then freeze in an ice-cream maker according to manufacturer’s directions. 

The photo unfortunately doesn’t capture the flavor, but honest to goodness this was the best peach ice cream we’ve ever had (and yes, we are including full-on rich dairy ice cream in this statement).

Stay tuned for more peach goodness.  Oh, and of course we’ll be pairing peaches with Georgia pecans!

Introduction
