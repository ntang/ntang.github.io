Ballin’ (Frozen Melon Balls & Grapesicles)

I love melon balls.  You’ll see them time after time on this blog in bento boxes, on party trays and in all sorts of fruit sculptures on various holidays.

In the summer my favorite way to serve melon balls is frozen, and here are 5 reasons why they excite me: 

  • they’re a refreshing snack in and of themselves
  • they can act as an ice pack for other perishables in a brown bag lunch or in a picnic basket
  • they’re awesome when tossed into a smoothie to make the drink colder and the consistency more smoothie-like
  • freezing fruit is a food-saving measure if you don’t think you’ll get around to polishing off a melon before it spoils. 
  • frozen melon balls can be used in lieu of regular ice cubes in cocktails, seltzer, fruit juices, etc.

Also in the spotlight today are frozen grapes.  These little gems have long been a favorite summertime snack of my children, who also love any sort of food on a stick.  Voila!  Grapesicles (inspired by a photograph from the now-defunct veganfamilyinc.com).

Think about it:  what child wouldn’t want to come inside after sweating up a storm on a hot summer day and be greeted with a cool, refreshing (and healthy!) frozen treat? 

One word of advice:  if your children are on the younger side, opt for lollipop sticks (plastic or paper) instead of wooden kebabs with their pointy tips.

Pizza Friday: Pizza Dumplings!

Homemade pizza dough stuffed with a dollop of fat-free and part-skim ricotta cheese (50/50 mix), a spoonful of marinara sauce, low-fat mozzarella and vegan pepperoni; extra marinara sauce for dipping (low-sodium / no sugar added); fresh strawberries.

The Decorated Cookie provided the inspiration for this week’s Pizza Friday post.  We initially intended to make the mini calzone lollipops here,  but the girls wanted to put their own spin on things by leaving the stick off and renaming them “pizza dumplings.”  Since we try to involve the kids in our culinary escapades as much as possible, who were we to argue?

Note:  we made our own pizza dough late-night on Thursday, and figured out way too late in the game that we didn’t have any yeast on hand.  My husband chose this recipe to go with, and while we usually try to limit our use of white flour as much as possible, we didn’t want to push our luck by going yeast-free *and* using wheat flour.  Maybe we were overly cautious and the end result would have been fine (still in the learning process here), but with no back-up planned we decided to play it safe.

If you go the mini calzone on a stick route, wouldn’t they look amazing on a buffet table arranged in a vase?  They will definitely be served at my stepson’s 16th birthday party in August.

Simple, Sensible “Sushi”

Short grain “sticky rice,” chicken cutlet and julienned granny smith apple rolled in a soy wrapper; steamed & shelled edamame (soybeans); watermelon balls

The last time we had a single leftover chicken cutlet (baked, with an Egg Beater wash, low-fat bread crumbs and a drizzle of olive oil) we diced it up and used it as a pizza topping as a “Pizza Friday” option.  This time around we saw it as a great way to test out the naturally colorful “Sushi Party Soy Wrappers” from Yamamotoyama that we purchased on Amazon (here).  Sushi rolls are relatively simple to make as long as you have a sushi rolling mat like this one.

While we have transitioned from white rice to brown pretty much all across the board, I have yet to find a brown rice that I like in sushi, and the kids agree.  I’d love to hear some suggestions if you have a particular type or brand you love.

Pizza Friday: Whole Wheat Personal Pizza

Whole wheat pizza dough with no-sugar-added/low-sodium tomato sauce and low-fat mozzarella cheese; red seedless grapes; crispin apple slices

Oops, this week’s pizza was just a little too large for the box, so we had to cut it up after the shot was taken. 

How we pack our pizza each week:

  • pizza is baked in oven
  • pizza is then wrapped in tin foil with top open and reheated in toaster oven just before departure
  • pizza comes out of toaster oven and 4 tooth picks that have been clipped with using a scissor (they must be shortened to fit in bento) are inserted to keep cheese from sticking to foil
  • tin foil is folded over and pizza is placed in bento
  • insert entire bento into insulated sleeve

Happy Friday!

Puzzled Over What to Make for Lunch?

Cream cheese (50/50 mix of fat-free and 1/3 less fat) and fresh strawberry slices on 7 grain bread with flax and whole grain white bread; crudites (raw carrots, cucumber and red pepper slices) with hummus; granny smith apple slices; pistachios

Today’s lunch was one that had to be simple and neat enough to eat “on the road,” as the girls will be picked up before lunch time at school and whisked away to their doctor’s appointment in Manhattan.  I didn’t want to pack any forks or spoons or anything remotely messy that would end up on a waiting room floor — just finger foods.

It is also possible that they will eat their lunches under the watchful eye of their nutritionist (say “cheese!”), so I wanted to appear as though we’re on our best nutritional behavior.  Which we have been.  Most of the time.  You guys will be my witnesses, right?

Oh wait, do you think the white whole grain bread will end up on her radar?  Maybe this wasn’t a good day to introduce the Lunch Punch puzzle sandwich press after all.  We purchased ours on Amazon.com, and look forward to mastering the technique when we’re not in a rush and doing more extensive puzzles for school and home party platters.  Check out the photos on the link to see some major coolness in action.
