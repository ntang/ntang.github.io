Skillet Pineapple Upside Down Cake with Ginger & Citrus

Made in honor of my 16 year old stepson (who fancies pineapple and key lime pie like nobody’s business), this cake is a twist on the classic pineapple upside down cake and features hints of ginger and citrus.

For the bottom (which will become the top):

  • 8 slices of organic pineapple in natural juice (see below for what to do with the rest of the can or container of juice from fresh, cored pineapple)
  • 2 tablespoons of high quality butter
  • 1/8 of a cup of organic brown sugar
  • dye-free / preservative free maraschino cherries (we used Tillen Farms brand)
  • optional:  a few pecan pieces 

The Cake Batter:

  • 3 pastured eggs
  • 3/8 of a cup of local honey
  • 1/4 cup of buttermilk or kefir
  • 1 cup of whole wheat pastry flour
  • 1 tablespoon of ginger
  • 1 1/2 teaspoon aluminum-free baking powder
  • 1 tablespoon of real vanilla

The Glaze:

  • the juice from can or fresh pineapple container
  • about 1/4 cup of freshly squeezed Meyer lemon juice (could use regular lemon or lime instead)

For the method, please click here.  Thank you to Ozark Homesteader for the inspiration!

Better for You Brownies

My youngest stepson turned 12 last week, and for his birthday party he requested brownies (note: as odd as it sounds, he does not like cake — brownies are his go-to birthday treat).

The recipe we used this year is called “Better for You Brownies” and it came to us via our school Wellness Committee.  The eventual plan is to assemble a collection of recipes and to distribute a cookbook to all the parents and students - something I am looking very much looking forward to participating in (both in contributing recipes and acquiring new ones).

My husband tweaked the original recipe to reduce the prune aftertaste down to nil.  Here is his updated version of these moist and delicious non-traditional brownies:

  • 3/4 cup prunes
  • 1/4 cup no-sugar-added organic apple juice, warmed
  • 1 cup filtered water, divided
  • 2 1/2 cups light brown sugar (unpacked)
  • 6 ounces of bittersweet dark chocolate (we used 70% cacao)
  • 4 large organic eggs
  • 1 tablespoon of organic vanilla extract
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 3/4 cup whole wheat flour
  • 2 tablespoons of wheat germ (we used Bob’s Red Mill)
  • 8 tablespoons of cacao powder (we used Valrhona brand)
  • 1/8th of a teaspoon of cinnamon
  • 1/8th of a teaspoon of cayenne (will still be child friendly — don’t worry)
  • 1 teaspoon of kosher salt
  • 1 teaspoon of baking powder

Preheat oven to 325F.  Spray a 9 x 13 pan with cooking spray.  Soak prunes in the warm apple juice for 30 minutes.  Transfer prune mixture to a food processor, add 1/2 cup of water and puree (do not leave any chunks whatsoever).

In a saucepan over low heat, add the light brown sugar and 1/2 cup water and bring to a simmer.  Remove from the heat and whisk in the bittersweet chocolate, prune puree and the eggs.  Wisk the flours, wheat germ, cocoa powder, kosher salt and baking powder.

Stir in vanilla, cinnamon and cayenne.

Pour into the prepared pan and bake 35 to 40 minutes.  Cool.

Yield:  24 (2 inch) brownies

My Girlfriend Laura’s Southern Cornbread

Below:  Grilled free-range, antibiotic-free breast of chicken (cut into dipping strips); mini sweet orange & red bell peppers (de-cored and seeded after photo was taken); corn bread (as a special treat); fruit salad of cherries, green seedless grapes, red seedless grapes and a slice of peach; dipping sauce for chicken in the condiment holder

While this cornbread recipe isn’t healthy per se (with the white flour and sugar it falls under the special treat category), it packs far less sugar and fat than traditional Southern cornbread without compromising much on taste or texture. 

Laura was gracious enough to bake this version of her cornbread for my family when we stayed at her home in Georgia earlier this summer.  It was a huge hit among the adults and children alike — all 11 of us.

  •  Melt 1/2 cup of butter and whisk in:
  •   2/3 cup of sugar, and:
  • 3 egg whites, 1 whole egg
  • Then stir in 2 cups of low-fat buttermilk and:
  • 1 teaspoon of aluminum-free baking soda

Set aside above mixture. In a separate bowl, mix together:

  • 2 cups of cornmeal (Laura sent me home with local, organic Georgia cornmeal)
  • 2 cups of flour
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 1 ear of de-kerneled corn on the cob

Mix wet ingredients with dry ingredients until combined and big lumps/dry spots are no longer apparent.

Pout into greased (use cooking spray of choice) 9” x 13” baking pan and bake approximately 30-35 minutes at 375 degrees (check center with toothpick).

Mix wet ingredients with dry ingredients until combined and big lumps/dry spots are no longer apparent.

Pour into greased (use cooking spray of choice) 9” x 13” baking pan and bake approximately 30-25 minutes at 375 degrees (check center with toothpick).

Whole Wheat Banana Muffins with Dark Chocolate Chips

In mini form, these whole wheat banana muffins with dark chocolate chips would be a great celebratory snack for the classroom.  For a breakfast treat or after-school / anytime snack, substitute the chocolate chips for walnuts, or just make them plain. 

The muffins were inspired by this recipe for low-fat banana bread from Andrea’s Wellness Notes.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup sugar (use 3/4 cup if you aren’t using the dark chocolate chips)
  • 1/2 cup apple sauce
  • 2 eggs(we used Egg Beaters and the result was fantastic on day 1.  The next the texture was slightly off but still more than acceptable)
  • 1 Tbsp. vanilla
  • 4 very ripe bananas, smashed, but still chunky
  • 3 Tsp. baking powder
  • 1 cup whole wheat flour
  • 1 cup all purpose flour

Directions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees.
  2. Pour the sugar and apple sauce in a bowl and mix well.
  3. Add the eggs and vanilla and mix.
  4. Add the smashed bananas.
  5. In a separate bowl, combine the baking powder and flour and mix well.
  6. Add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients plus optional dark chocolate chips or walnuts and mix.
  7. Pour the batter into cupcake liners.
  8. Bake for 35 minutes, then check until a toothpick inserted comes out clean.


Servings: 12

Very Cherry Ice

The girls are taking this archived lunch in their bentos today, so we thought we’d share a new recipe we came across and renamed Very Cherry Ice (a dye-free, preservative-free natural alternative to an Italian ice and a refreshing special treat on a scorcher of an afternoon).

Serves 6

Bring 1 lb fresh cherries (stemmed and pitted), 1 cup of filtered water, 1/3 cup sugar and juice of 1/2 lime to boil in pan.  Simmer until cherries soften, about 8 minutes.  Cool for 10 minutes.  Puree mixture in batches in a blender or food processor; pour into 9-inch baking pan.  Chill until cold, 1 hour.  Cover and freeze until frozen around edges, 1 hour.  With fork, stir frozen chunks toward center.  Repeat, stirring every 30 minutes (until frozen solid), 3 hours.  To serve, scrape across surface with fork, transfering ice to 6 dishes.

Note:  we altered the recipe above by cutting the sugar back to 1/4 cup.  We also added the juice of 1/2 of a lemon to make it more tart.  I strongly recommend that you do this if you try the recipe yourself.

Recipe from Weight Watchers Magazine, July/August 2011 issue, page 153 (called “Fresh Cherry Ice”)

Banana & Chocolate Hazelnut Crepe

Whole wheat crepe brushed with a light coating of (palm oil free) organic chocolate hazelnut spread and stuffed with banana slices; fresh organic strawberries; cucumber and red pepper salad

We all have cravings, so the goal today was to find a way to satisfy those cravings while still adhering to a semblance of healthy guidelines whenever possible (sometimes it’s not, and that’s OK, too) — the “lesser of the evils,” if you will, without compromising the “yum” factor.  For our banana and chocolate hazelnut crepes we:

  • used whole wheat instead of white flour
  • substituted an organic, palm oil-free chocolate hazelnut spread instead of using the popular national brand
  • used half a serving size of spread (thinned out with heat and brushed across the crepe with a basting brush), therefore cutting the saturated fat content down to 1 gram of fat and 8.5 grams of sugar)
  • loaded the crepe with as much fresh fruit as possible 

Délicieux!

French Toast for Lunch? Oh Yeah!

Whole wheat French toast triangles stuffed with natural, low-sugar organic peanut butter and banana slices, paired with an organic nitrate-free turkey sausage and whole strawberries.  The condiment box contains 1 1/2 tablespoons of real organic New York State maple syrup.  Toast was made with an eggbeater wash.

My husband and I decided that neither of us should have to cook Valentine’s Day dinner, but ordering take-out for our family can be a real challenge.  In general, take-out from restaurants is far less healthy than home-cooked meals because of the extra sodium, oils/butter, etc., but if there is a restrictive diet involved, it becomes all the more challenging.  We do have a couple of stand-bys (our local pizzeria will make us a pie with half the oils and mozzarella cheese, for instance), but we wanted something a little more special.  That’s when we remembered Jennie Pineda!

Jennie runs a home delivery service called JENNIE’S KITCHEN, and caters to families who want healthy, organic home-cooked meals.  She will also entertain almost any diet, so when we called she was happy to hold the saturated fats and cholesterol.  Our menu was divine.  Among the offerings were soft tacos stuffed with grilled salmon and a granny smith, cucumber and cilantro salsa, mini shredded chicken burritos with tomatoes and hidden carrots and whole wheat quesadillas with reduced fat cheeses.  We actually ordered the french toast triangles for dessert originally (we decided to put the smores we posted about yesterday on hold due to time constraints), but since they weren’t overly sweet, we thought that the girls could pack the leftovers in their boxes as a special treat for lunch.  They were happy to oblige!

Jennie Pineda can be reached at jennieskitchen@gmail.com  Drop her a line if you’d like to see a sample menu.  It’s good stuff!
