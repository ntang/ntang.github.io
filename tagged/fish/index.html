Confused About Which Fish to Eat?

Fresh, local flounder fillets cut into neat rectangles, dipped in an egg wash (pastured eggs, please) and dredged in seasoned whole wheat bread crumbs and ground flax seed.  Simply drizzle with olive oil and bake!  I make these in bulk and freeze for school lunches.  Use low-sugar ketchup as a dipping sauce and serve with whole organic carrots and organic cherry tomatoes.

No waste tip:  after rectangles are formed, cook all remaining “irregular shaped” pieces of fish and refrigerate leftovers for a fish taco night!  Note to self:   pick up some limes…

A Moroccan Diversion

Grilled whole Branzino rubbed with olive oil, salt and pepper and stuffed with lemon slices, fresh thyme, bay leaves.

Chermoula sauce (olive oil, cilantro, garlic, parsley, lemon, smokey paprika, cumin, ginger, chili flakes, coriander seeds, salt blended together in a mini food processor)

Vegetable tagine (chick peas, butternut squash, onions, carrots, tomatoes, vegetable broth, cinnamon, cumin, saffron, turmeric, salt, pepper, coriander powder) over couscous

Grilled skinless chicken drumsticks

My husband and I were in the mood for an evening out at a fancy restaurant, but we couldn’t declare an official date night for a myriad of reasons.  So, we decided to invite my father-in-law over and make a fancy meal ourselves, at home. 

In the spirit of having the children try new foods (or at least be exposed to them in close proximity, which is often how these experiments work) we decided to cook exactly what the adults were craving, plus a plate of grilled chicken drumsticks in case the whole Branzino (with eyes and tail intact) became a bit too much for the younger kids to bear.

I’d be lying if I were to say they tried everything and loved it, but it went surprisingly well with no complaints from any of the 4 kids.  Most ate the plain couscous around the edges, though my teen stepson was taken off guard by how good the vegetable tagine was (he was never a fan of squash before).  The 3 younger kids opted for raw veggies with their drumsticks instead of the tagine.

Interestingly enough, all 4 kids acknowledged how beautiful the food looked, and we talked about how dishes like whole fish remind us where our food comes from, and how it hopefully evokes a feeling of respect and appreciation.

Today’s 6th Birthday Request

Homemade breaded flounder fillet sandwich on whole wheat bread swimming in a sea of blueberries with a yellow pepper sun; anjou pear and cantaloupe balls; Goldfish pretzels and whole grain baked cheddar Goldfish.  The fish was made with whole wheat breadcrumbs and was baked with a drizzle of olive oil.

Today’s lunch was requested and designed by the Little Ladies, one of whom is celebrating her 6th birthday today.  Unfortunately, we failed to execute the use of fancy carved carrots as coral.  Next time!

Happy Birthday, my lovely little girl.  You were longed for since I was a wee lass of about 3, and I’m thankful for you every day.
