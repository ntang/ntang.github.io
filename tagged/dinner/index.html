BLT on Whole Wheat Challah Bread

Humanely-raised, nitrate-free crispy turkey bacon, mixed spring lettuce, beef steak tomato and avocado slices (in lieu of mayo) on whole wheat challah bread; sweet corn; baby spinach with carrot and red raw pepper slices; plum balls and red seedless grapes

Don’t stop at lunch — we enthusiastically recommend BLTs as a quick and easy make-ahead summertime dinner.  Just cook whatever bacon you’ll be using in advance (we love all types of bacon — the Applegate Farms brand of turkey crisps up nicely when cooked in the oven, but we also like pork bacon and even duck bacon, all nitrate/antibiotic-free varieties, of course) and assemble the sandwich just in time for the meal.  Serve with healthy fruit and veggie sides. 

If your kids don’t fancy the idea of eating anything besides bacon on their sandwich, try a deconstructed version.  If you are a grain-free household, a BLT salad is also insanely delicious.

Make-in-Bulk Homemade Chicken Strips

Homemade chicken strips/fingers/nuggets are a perfect make-ahead-and-freeze item for use in back-to-school bentos (or for a quick and easy dinner after a busy day).

Our recipe varies from batch to batch, but this is how we did yesterday’s supply:

Ingredients:

  • free-range, antibiotic-free chicken breasts cut into strips/nuggets
  • organic egg wash (we used a ratio of 3 egg whites to one whole egg, but feel feel to use whole eggs or Egg Beaters)
  • breading = a mixture of whole wheat bread crumbs, bran, and a bit of flax seed meal (use a 50/50 mix of whole wheat and regular bread crumbs if you are just beginning to transition away from using white flour)

Simply drag the chicken through the egg wash, then through the breading mixture, then either pan fry with a bit of olive oil or drizzle with olive oil and bake.

We then froze the strips side by side (not touching) for a short amount of time, then transferred the chicken to a freezer-safe bag. 

Our past bento box examples:

(Above) Homemade Chicken Tenders & Friendly Fries:  organic chicken tenders paired with homemade baked French fries seasoned with salt, pepper, rosemary and thyme; golden delicious apple slices; dried apricots


(Above) Baked chicken katsu, brown rice, steamed edamame with sea salt; fruit salad of pineapple and raspberries.


You can use the same recipe to make homemade fish sticks (ours are above)!  Super yummy and healthy.

Whole Wheat Ravioli with Spinach Soy Ricotta & Fresh Pesto

Our garden basil literally looked like a scene out of Jack and the Beanstalk after returning from vacation, so the Little Ladies got to work with a pair of scissors and cut 2 packed cups of basil for use in a fresh pesto sauce.  The recipe is very simple, and can be found here.

We decided to serve the pesto over mini whole wheat ravioli with spinach soy ricotta that we purchased from the frozen food section of Whole Foods.  We originally purchased it as an emergency stash for nights when we were too tired to spend 5 hours making fresh ravioli (OK, you caught me.  We’ve never actually made our own pasta… yet — we actually purchased it because it sounded lactose-free).

Thankfully, my lactose-intolerant husband took a closer look at the nutritional information before he indulged and low and behold, there was also real cheese inside. 2 lactose pills later, he survived without much discomfort.

Our habit of reading labels paid off once again.

Note:  I don’t usually fib to the children about what they’re eating (I also don’t hide things inside of their food), but since they historically only eat their spinach raw I pretended that the green stuff inside the ravioli was more basil.  They loved it, which doesn’t surprise me.   

All in all, a tasty meal.
