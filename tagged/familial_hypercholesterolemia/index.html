Turkey on Whole Wheat Challah

Nitrate/antibiotic-free turkey breast slices on whole wheat Challah bread; organic golden raspberries; local gala apple slices

The Little Ladies love turkey breast and request it fairly frequently.  We keep things exciting by alternating what type of bread the sandwich is made on (think whole wheat Challah (above), a multi-grain wrap, whole grain raisin bread, hearty spelt rolls with dried cranberries from the local greenmarket, etc.) or we serve the turkey without bread in the form of deli roll-ups or kebabs.  

Simple adjustments can make an ordinary packed lunch seem special.

Make-in-Bulk Homemade Chicken Strips

Homemade chicken strips/fingers/nuggets are a perfect make-ahead-and-freeze item for use in back-to-school bentos (or for a quick and easy dinner after a busy day).

Our recipe varies from batch to batch, but this is how we did yesterday’s supply:

Ingredients:

  • free-range, antibiotic-free chicken breasts cut into strips/nuggets
  • organic egg wash (we used a ratio of 3 egg whites to one whole egg, but feel feel to use whole eggs or Egg Beaters)
  • breading = a mixture of whole wheat bread crumbs, bran, and a bit of flax seed meal (use a 50/50 mix of whole wheat and regular bread crumbs if you are just beginning to transition away from using white flour)

Simply drag the chicken through the egg wash, then through the breading mixture, then either pan fry with a bit of olive oil or drizzle with olive oil and bake.

We then froze the strips side by side (not touching) for a short amount of time, then transferred the chicken to a freezer-safe bag. 

Our past bento box examples:

(Above) Homemade Chicken Tenders & Friendly Fries:  organic chicken tenders paired with homemade baked French fries seasoned with salt, pepper, rosemary and thyme; golden delicious apple slices; dried apricots


(Above) Baked chicken katsu, brown rice, steamed edamame with sea salt; fruit salad of pineapple and raspberries.


You can use the same recipe to make homemade fish sticks (ours are above)!  Super yummy and healthy.

My Girlfriend Laura’s Low-Fat Southern Cornbread

Below:  Grilled free-range, antibiotic-free breast of chicken (cut into dipping strips); mini sweet orange & red bell peppers (de-cored and seeded after photo was taken); corn bread (as a special treat); fruit salad of cherries, green seedless grapes, red seedless grapes and a slice of peach; dipping sauce for chicken in the condiment holder

While this cornbread recipe isn’t healthy per se (with the white flour and sugar it falls under the special treat category), it packs far less sugar and fat than traditional Southern cornbread without compromising much on taste or texture. 

Laura was gracious enough to bake this version of her cornbread for my family when we stayed at her home in Georgia earlier this summer.  It was a huge hit among the adults and children alike — all 11 of us.

  •  Melt 1/2 cup of Smart Balance and whisk in:
  •   2/3 cup of sugar, and:
  • 1 cup Egg Beaters
  • Then stir in 2 cups of low-fat buttermilk and:
  • 1 teaspoon of baking soda

Set aside above mixture. In a separate bowl, mix together:

  • 2 cups of cornmeal (Laura sent me home with local, organic Georgia cornmeal)
  • 2 cups of flour
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 1 ear of de-kerneled corn on the cob

Mix wet ingredients with dry ingredients until combined and big lumps/dry spots are no longer apparent.

Pout into greased (use cooking spray of choice) 9” x 13” baking pan and bake approximately 30-35 minutes at 375 degrees (check center with toothpick).

Mix wet ingredients with dry ingredients until combined and big lumps/dry spots are no longer apparent.

Pour into greased (use cooking spray of choice) 9” x 13” baking pan and bake approximately 30-25 minutes at 375 degrees (check center with toothpick).

Back-to-School Check List

I am certainly not trying to will the rest of the summer away, but with 4 weeks left of vacation it’s time to start giving some thought to what lies ahead.  Here is my rough outline of a game plan:

Pictured above:  Organic plain fat-free Greek yogurt; Early Bird brand fresh locally-made granola (organic rolled oats, toasted organic coconut, organic pepitas, organic sunflower seeds, fancy pecans, real maple syrup, extra-virgin olive oil, salt) and a fruit salad consisting of fresh mango, strawberries, red seedless grapes (halved) and gala apples.


  • Order bento boxes if needed (we have historically used Laptop Lunches on this site — see HERE for our own example  — but may experiment with other brands this year).   Note: Thermos Funtainers are great for soups or other hot meals.  They have been tested and can safely store hot foods up to 6 hours.
  • Organize  work space.  Our workspace is a butcher block kitchen island with shelving and it has foil wrap, decorative picks, various napkins (we pack disposable and cloth, although the Little Ladies seem to prefer wiping their hands on their clothing so the napkins often return in pristine shape), cookie cutters, our bento boxes and the accompanying containers, eating utensils and water bottles all at the ready so there is no last-minute fumbling.  
  • Menu plan.  Try to forecast your week, and a day or 2 before school starts have your ingredients at the ready (mise en place technique) and in the refrigerator ready to go to save time.

The Little Ladies and I are gearing up this month for a heck of a freezing spree in preparation for back-to-school.  Back to school is a hectic time in almost every household, and ours is no exception.  If you only knew!  Here are some of the items on our hit list that we will use as needed for the lunchboxes, breakfast or even dinner. 

  • 3 dozen mini turkey meatballs
  • 2 large containers of homemade tomato sauce
  • 1 large tray of low-fat, whole wheat lasagna (with a few mini-versions for lunchboxes)
  • dozens of homemade chicken fingers and nuggets and fish sticks (we use an egg beater wash, whole wheat bread crumbs, seasoning and a bit of bran to bulk up the fiber and then either bake or lightly fry in olive oil)

  • healthed-up French Toast dippers (we’re going to incorporate flax into the mix)
  • a variety of healthy mini muffins

Now is a great time to invest in a new set of greener freezer-ware if you haven’t already.

We are also going to re-stock the pantry with the following:

  • whole wheat pastry flour, coconut flour, various grains like quinoa and brown rice
  • a variety of whole wheat pasta
  • healthy cereals that are low in sugar and sodium and that taste great
  • steel cut oatmeal (save yourself the time and hassles by cooking it in a rice steamer set on timer)

Introduction

Little Ladies Who Lunch was inspired by a diagnosis of FAMILIAL HYPERCHOLESTEROLEMIA for 3 out of 6 household members (myself and my 2 young daughters) just days before the start of the 2010-2011 school year.

The panic that ensued quickly gave way to "when life gives you lemons, make lemonade." And preserved lemons. And lemon roasted chicken. And low-fat lemon yogurt cake. You get the picture.
