Organic Chicken Noodle Soup & a Summer Sandwich

One of the Little Ladies has been down for the count with a Strep infection (plus a virus on top of the Strep!) for the past 4 days.  We’re all a little sleep deprived, but she’s finally on the road to recovery.  Her request today?  Homemade chicken noodle soup. I was delighted to make it for her (for all of us, really).  Here’s how I did it:

  • 32 ounces (1 quart) of organic, low-sodium chicken stock  (feel free to use vegetable stock if you prefer)
  • 64 ounces of filtered water 
  • 2 celery sticks, finely chopped
  • 1 parsnip, chopped
  • 2 large carrots, chopped
  • 1 turnip, whole
  • 1 leek, finely chopped
  • 1/2 a whole vidalia onion
  • 1 whole 6 pound organic, free-range chicken (broken down before serving)
  • whole wheat “egg noodles”
  • 3 cloves of garlic, whole
  • salt and pepper to taste
  • garnish of fresh parsley

I also decided to insert a little summer into the meal by making an avocado and Jersey tomato sandwich on a whole wheat English muffin with a dab of olive oil mayonnaise and broccoli sprouts. The Ladies wouldn’t touch it, but my husband and I gobbled our sandwiches right up.

Delicious and satisfying.

Now to get some rest.

Introduction

Little Ladies Who Lunch was inspired by a diagnosis of FAMILIAL HYPERCHOLESTEROLEMIA for 3 out of 6 household members (myself and my 2 young daughters) just days before the start of the 2010-2011 school year.

The panic that ensued quickly gave way to "when life gives you lemons, make lemonade." And preserved lemons. And lemon roasted chicken. And low-fat lemon yogurt cake. You get the picture.
