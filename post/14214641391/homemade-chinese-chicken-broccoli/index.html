Homemade Chinese Chicken & Broccoli

Homemade leftover Chinese chicken and broccoli over brown rice; organic Granny Smith apple slices; a clementine “stuffed” with pomegranate seeds

I’m all about festive Christmas colors right now.  I can’t help myself! 

Today’s bento features last night’s homemade Chinese chicken and broccoli leftovers.  The dish is simple to prepare and tastes a million times better than the chicken and broccoli you’d find at the local take-out joint, and it’s likely to be a lot healthier, too. 

Admittingly, the back and forth with the wok can be a bit dizzying until you get used to it, but forge ahead and you’ll be very pleased with the results.

The secret to avoiding “white girl stir fry” as I affectionately like to call it is to velvet the chicken first.  The full set of instructions is below.  Please give it a try!

Chinese Chicken & Broccoli

First, velvet the chicken. To do this, mix together

  • egg whites from 2 large, organic eggs
  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt
  • 2 tablespoons of corn starch

Pour the mixture over cubed, humanely raised/antibiotic-free chicken (I used 1.5 pounds) that has been placed in a bowl and mix well, taking care to thoroughly cover each chicken cube.  Cover with plastic wrap and let sit in the fridge for at least 30 minutes — longer if you can.

While your chicken is velveting, prep your sauce:

  • 2 tablespoons oyster sauce
  • 8 tablespoons light (reduced sodium) soy sauce
  • 8 tablespoons filtered water
  • 2 cloves of minced garlic
  • grated ginger to taste

You’ll be able to adjust the sauce to your own liking eventually, but this is a good base. 

In another small bowl, prepare thickener and then combine with the above sauce:

2 teaspoons of cornstarch mixed with 2 tablespoons of filtered water — thoroughly blended (I use a mini whisk). 

1.  wash and drain the broccoli (I used 2 bunches). Cut the stalk diagonally into thin slices. Cut the flowerets into 3 or 4 pieces and set aside.

2.  Heat 1/3rd of a cup of canola oil (organic, expeller-pressed, if possible) in a wok (around 275 degrees if you want to be technical).  Add the chicken cubes, and let cook until they just turn white all over, using a wooden spoon or chopsticks to gently separate them. Quickly remove the chicken cubes from the wok as soon as they turn white, and drain in a colander or on paper towels.

3.  Drain the oil out of the wok or preheat a second wok on medium high to high heat. Add 2 tablespoons oil. When the oil is hot, add the crushed garlic and stir fry for 10 seconds.

4.  Add the broccoli, sprinkle salt and sugar over to taste, and stir fry briefly, turning down the heat if necessary to make sure it doesn’t burn. Add the 1 cup water, and cook the broccoli, covered, for 4 - 5 minutes, until it turns a bright green and is tender but still crisp. Remove from the wok and drain.

5. Clean out the wok and heat 2 more tablespoons oil. Add the broccoli and the chicken together, stirring and tossing to cook the chicken through. Add the sauce and cornstarch mixture in the middle of the wok and stir quickly to thicken. Mix everything together and serve hot over brown rice.
