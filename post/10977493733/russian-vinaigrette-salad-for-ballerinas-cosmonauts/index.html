Russian Vinaigrette Salad for Ballerinas & Cosmonauts

Russian Vinaigrette salad is a wildly popular dish in Eastern Europe and is a staple at most celebratory meals.  The variations vary from region to region, but below you’ll find the version we make in our home.

This salad is not only delicious and chock-full of healthy veggies, but it is absolutely beautiful to look at.  It’s certainly worth seeing if your little ballerina or cosmonaut will indulge (and if not — take heart.  Most adults I’ve introduced to the salad give it rave reviews even if they were never fans of beets to begin with). 

RUSSIAN VINAIGRETTE SALAD


Ingredients:

  • fresh beets – 2-3 medium
  • whole carrots – 3 large
  • yukon gold potatoes - 2 medium
  • crunchy kosher dills - 3
  • 1 can of sweet peas
  • red onion - 1 medium
  • extra virgin olive oil – high quality, roughly 4 tablespoons 
  • salt and pepper – to taste
  • parsley - as garnish

How to prepare:

  • Boil (with skins on!) beets, potatoes, carrots in one pot until beet is tender enough to pierce easily with a fork (check after 25 minutes)
  • Drain water, then cool and peel off skins.  They should come off very easily when cooled.
  • Get ready for some serious chopping. 

Chop beets into very small, diced pieces and place in a bowl.

We recommend parsley as garnish to give the salad a pop of green, but we didn’t have any on hand.  You could also serve in a groovy platter with small squares of dark bread around it. 

Enjoy!
