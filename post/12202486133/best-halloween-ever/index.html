“Best Halloween Ever”

Our spooky Halloween Hydration Station ran without a hitch and ended up being a major hit among our trick-or-treaters.  When all was said and done, we served just over 6 gallons of filtered tap water (or “Swamp Water” as our signage said) to neighborhood kids in small, eco-friendly cups.  Truth be told, we also had a stash of chilled prosecco under the table for deserving parents, but that’s another post (next year we’ll have “boos” and brews for sure!). 

In the interest of full disclosure, I also had a major case of last minute jitters knowing full well the connection between Halloween “food” and emotions.   On the day-of I did actively seek out a supply of candy, pretzels (and to our credit — granola bars and clementines) just in case the Hydration Station was a flop.  Alas, even the kids who didn’t indulge in our “Swamp Water” (our Brita could barely keep up with us as the demand grew throughout the evening) really seemed to genuinely love the clementines best!

It was an amazing experiment and the verdict is in:  we’re doing our Halloween Hydration Station again next year — no candy necessary.  To the contrary, the overall feeling in the air was that the kids loved having the opportunity to quench their thirst and found the whole set-up to be unique and well, much needed. 

As far as our own kids go, we laid out a bunch of ideas for them, and also presented the option of rejecting all of those ideas in favor of the post WW2 tradition of collecting commercially made candy and consuming it gradually over time.  

Here is what they chose:

Daughters, ages 6 and 8:  Went out with me the night before Halloween and picked out locally-made artisanal dark chocolate bars for themselves.  They were told that they could have anything in the store, and after agonizing for what seemed like 30 minutes, re-emerged with their 2 (full size) choices.  They both stated with absolute certainty that they wanted the experience of trick or treating and wouldn’t miss it for the world, and I can’t say I blame them.  I love trick-or-treating myself!  Since they genuinely prefer the taste of good quality chocolate over the commercially made big brands like Hershey’s, for them trick or treating is more about the experience of seeing how much loot they can collect as they take in all of the decorations and costumes.  Once their bags were fully loaded after doing the circuit with their dad, they came to help out at the family “Hydration Station” and had great fun doing so.  My younger daughter later turned her trick or treating stash over to the “Switch Witch” and will receive a doll she’s been eying in return, while my older daughter opted to trade her stash in to a local dentist who buys back candy and then sends it to our troops overseas. 

Stepsons, ages 11 and 16 (and his 16 year old friend): My 11 year old went out trick or treating with his middle school chum.  He came home with a loot bag that would rival the fullness of one of my laundry baskets, and is still mulling over what to do with it.  As of this morning, he was thinking of keeping “a few pieces” and then following my 8 year old’s example by participating in our local dentist’s buy-back plan.  The teens didn’t trick or treat (though they did have some fun at the Hydration Station for a bit), but instead patiently awaited dessert, which was from-scratch dark chocolate pudding and whole wheat/oatmeal dark chocolate chip cookies made by my husband. 

Everyone felt completely satisfied with the way things turned out.  Warm.  Fuzzy.  “Best Halloween ever,” said my 8 year old.  Her opinion was echoed by all in the house.  And as for mama, there is nothing that warms my soul more than to slow down and put love into the things we do and build happy memories…without building an association with or a reliance on the gratuitous excess of a commercialism.  Without rushing and going through the motions and not remembering why on earth we’ve embarked on some sort of activity that doesn’t bring us anything that feels rewarding. 

It also helps that all of the kids woke up on time this morning and were happy and well-rested.  No preservative/food dye hangovers to speak of!

Introduction

The panic that ensued quickly gave way to "when life gives you lemons, make lemonade." And preserved lemons. And lemon roasted chicken. And lemon yogurt cake. You get the picture.
