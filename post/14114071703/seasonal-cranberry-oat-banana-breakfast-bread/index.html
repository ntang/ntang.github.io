Seasonal Cranberry Oat Banana Breakfast Bread

Yesterday was a lovely family day at our house.  We took a brisk walk around Prospect Park (3.53 miles on this particular route), played games, made hot mulled cider, baked ourselves a breakfast treat to wake up to on Monday morning and cooked a Sunday dinner of whole wheat pappardelle sprinkled with fresh parmesan cheese, turkey meatballs, tomato basil sauce and homemade garlic bread made from whole roasted garlic.  Afterward we snuck in a little bit of “A Christmas Carol.” 

Days like these are important to my husband and me, as they keep us focused on what matters to us most during the holiday season, far away from the excess that surrounds us all.

It was nice to wake up this morning to the fruits of our labor and treat ourselves to a little slice of the weekend, inspired by this recipe from A Cozy Kitchen. 

  • 1 cup of all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup of whole wheat pastry flour
  • 1/4 cup rolled oats, plus 1 handful for topping
  • 2 teaspoons of baking powder (aluminum free)
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda (aluminum free)
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/3 cup light buttermilk
  • 1 tablespoon of canola oil (organic, expeller-pressed, if possible)
  • 1 tablespoon of pure organic vanilla extract
  • 2 large, organic eggs
  • 1/2 cup light brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup Turbinado sugar
  • 3 over-ripe bananas (please don’t cut corners here — the must be over-ripe)
  • 1 cup of cranberries


1. Pre-heat oven to 325˚F. Lightly grease a 9x5-inch pan and dust with a tablespoon of flour.

2. In a medium bowl, mix together all-purpose flour, whole wheat pastry flour, rolled oats, baking powder, baking soda and salt.

3. In a measuring cup, measure out the buttermilk. To the buttermilk, add the vegetable oil and vanilla extract and mix until combined.

4. In a large bowl, using an electric mixer, beat eggs, light brown sugar and white sugar together until thick and pale, approximately 3-5 minutes. Mix in smashed banana and buttermilk mixture. In two batches, add flour mixture, mixing until just combined, being sure not to over-mix.

5. Fold in cranberries and transfer batter to prepared loaf pan. Top with a handful of rolled oats.

6. Bake quick bread for 50 minutes, or until a tester inserted into the center comes out clean. Invert bread onto a rack to cool. Cranberry Oatmeal Banana Bread will be moist up to 3-4 days. Wrap tightly in plastic wrap to preserve its freshness.
