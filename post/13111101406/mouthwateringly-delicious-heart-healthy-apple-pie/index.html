Mouthwateringly Delicious (& Heart Healthy) Apple Pie

I recently had the pleasure of meeting Cathy Elton of What Would Cathy Eat.com, a blog dedicated to Cathy’s culinary adventures post-coronary stents.  An admirer of her work, I was very pleasantly surprised to learn that she lives only a few short blocks from my home.  How cool is it that Cathy, despite dietary restrictions, has been able to create recipes that even your most staunch food lover would salivate over?        

Yes, when it comes to pies, “mouthwateringly delicious” and “heart healthy” hardly seem to go hand in hand.  But Cathy has nailed it.  I quote the biggest food naysayer in our family (my 16 year old stepson) upon taking a bite:  “Wow.  Oh….wow.” 

I urge you to give this recipe a shot.  It has become our go-to apple pie recipe in our house, and trust me — I was previously very much of the mindset that a great crust required lard. 

Cathy Elton’s Apple Pie

Filling:
2 1/2 lbs apples, peeled, cored and sliced (about 6 large apples — Northern Spy if you can get them)
1 tablespoon lemon juice
3/4 teaspoon cinnamon
1/4 teaspoon freshly ground nutmeg
2/3 cup sugar
2 1/2 tablespoons cornstarch

Crust:
2 2/3 cups flour, half all purpose and half whole wheat pastry flour
1 teaspoon salt
2/3 cup organic canola oil or high-oleic safflower oil
6 tablespoons fat-free organic milk (from grass-fed cows, preferable)
1 teaspoon milk and 1 teaspoon sugar, for brushing top crust

Preheat oven to 400 degrees.

Combine the apples, lemon juice, spices, sugar and corn starch in a large bowl.

Whisk the flour and salt in a medium mixing bowl. Pour the oil in a glass measuring cup and add the milk, without stirring. Pour this mixture into the flour and stir briefly, just until combined. Divide the dough in half and form two balls.

Place a 15″ long piece of wax paper on your work surface, putting a few drops of water under the corners of the paper to keep it from sliding around. Put one ball on the paper and press it into a 6-inch circle. Top with another piece of wax paper and roll it out with a rolling pin to a 12-inch circle. If your circle is uneven, simply tear off a piece from one part and add it to another – it’s easy to make repairs.

Remove the top sheet and turn the dough over into a 9-inch pie pan, pressing to remove any air pockets. Pour in the filling. Roll out the second disc between fresh wax paper and place it on top of the pie. Fold the top crust under the bottom all the way around, and crimp the edges. Cut some slits in the top, then brush very lightly with milk and sprinkle on a little sugar.

Bake at 400 degrees for 10 minutes, then reduce the heat to 350 and bake about 45-50 minutes, until the crust is lightly golden and the filling is bubbling. Cool 3 or more hours before serving.
