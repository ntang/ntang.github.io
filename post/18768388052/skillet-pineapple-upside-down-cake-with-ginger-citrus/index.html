Skillet Pineapple Upside Down Cake with Ginger & Citrus

Made in honor of my 16 year old stepson (who fancies pineapple and key lime pie like nobody’s business), this cake is a twist on the classic pineapple upside down cake and features hints of ginger and citrus.

For the bottom (which will become the top):

  • 8 slices of organic pineapple in natural juice (see below for what to do with the rest of the can or container of juice from fresh, cored pineapple)
  • 2 tablespoons of high quality butter
  • 1/8 of a cup of organic brown sugar
  • dye-free / preservative free maraschino cherries (we used Tillen Farms brand)
  • optional:  a few pecan pieces 

The Cake Batter:

  • 3 pastured eggs
  • 3/8 of a cup of local honey
  • 1/4 cup of buttermilk or kefir
  • 1 cup of whole wheat pastry flour
  • 1 tablespoon of ginger
  • 1 1/2 teaspoon aluminum-free baking powder
  • 1 tablespoon of real vanilla

The Glaze:

  • the juice from can or fresh pineapple container
  • about 1/4 cup of freshly squeezed Meyer lemon juice (could use regular lemon or lime instead)

For the method, please click here.  Thank you to Ozark Homesteader for the inspiration!
