Dark Chocolate Chunk Zucchini Poppers

Dry Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups whole wheat pasty flour
  • 1 1/2 cups cake flour (or all-purpose flour)
  • 2 teaspoons freshly ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon freshly ground nutmeg
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt

Wet Ingredients:

  • 2 large organic eggs ***room temperature***
  • 1/3 cup canola oil
  • 3/4 cup plain, organic yogurt
  • 1/3 cup buttermilk (we used light buttermilk)
  • 1 cup organic Turbinado sugar (or brown sugar firmly packed)
  • 2 teaspoons pure vanilla extract
  • 2 1/2 cups of finely grated zucchini
  • 4 oz of dark chocolate (don’t skimp and use low-grade chips!), chunked

1.  Preheat oven to 350 degrees. Grease pan/muffin tins/mini loaf pans or whatever cooking vessel you choose.

2.  In a bowl, sift together dry ingredients and set aside.

3.  In a large bowl, beat eggs until foamy; then beat in yogurt, buttermilk, oil, sugar and vanilla.  Stir in grated zucchini and dark chocolate.  Combine well.

4.  Fold flour mixture into the wet ingredients and stir well until combined.

5.  Spoon batter into cooking vessel of your choice.  Cook until toothpick inserted in center of bread comes up clean (except for dark chocolate).

6.  Remove from oven and cool 10 minutes.  Loosen sides and remove from vessel.  Cool loaf completely before cutting.

This recipe, which lends itself well to freezing, is from the brilliant folks at Simple Bites.  It can be made virtually any style, from a full-size loaf to mini loaves to muffins to just about anything. 

If you have an overabundance of zucchini, do try this.  Likewise if you have an overabundance of dark chocolate!
