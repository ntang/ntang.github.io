Guacamole Finally Made the Bento!

Grass-fed lean ground beef and a sprinkling of extra sharp white cheddar in a whole wheat soft shell taco; pomegranate seeds; tangelo orange slices; a dollop of organic reduced fat sour cream; organic stone-ground blue corn chips with sea salt; homemade guacamole


Oh happy day!  My husband and I are rabid homemade guacamole fans, so naturally it’s pretty much a staple at our home.  Every once in a blue moon the kids would dip in, make a “yucky face” and declare guacamole to be “inedible.”  Such heart-breakers kids can be.  They tried other people’s guacamole with the same response, mostly complaining that in addition to being inedible it was “too mushy” (something ours apparently wasn’t, partially because my husband and I like chunks of avocados).

Last night I was out of red onions, so in a desperate move I tried vidalia onions as a substitute.  Guess what?  They ate it!  Not only did they eat it, but they asked that I make a fresh batch this morning to take to school in their bento boxes!  Success!  And mommy got to eat any leftovers on toast for breakfast!  Sorry for the string of exclamation points, but darn it I’m excited!

When it comes to foods and kids, if at first you don’t succeed…try again.  Sometimes it’s a tweak so small as trading in red onions for sweet onions.

Here is our recipe:

  • ripe avocados
  • sea salt
  • crushed garlic
  • a bit of fresh cilantro
  • diced tomatoes
  • a little hot sauce (optional)
  • red onions (or, um….clearly vidalia onions work)
  • fresh squeezed lime juice
  • do not puree into oblivion.  Leave some chunks!

Oh, and if you’re wondering about the change in bento appearance today, my youngest is testing out her step-brother’s Planetbox while her sister remains loyal to Laptop Lunches.
